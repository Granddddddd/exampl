Cambridge

An old English university town and the county seat of Cambridgeshire, located about 50 miles (80 km) north of London and surrounded by a large number of smaller towns and villages. Also located in the heart of Silicon Fen, it has a reputation as the UK's leading high-tech centre, mainly due to the founding of Acorn Computers and Sinclair and because Cambridge is one of the main components of the Oxford-Cambridge Arc.

Manchester

The main city of Northern England, as well as the so-called North-West region of England. Manchester is the central city of the urban area known as Greater Manchester. The population of Manchester is 545,500[2]. Greater Manchester has a population of 2,702,209[3] The metropolis is the third most populous in Great Britain, as well as the second British center of culture and art after London. The city is located near the western slope of the Pennines, on the river Irwell[en]. Its importance as an important transport hub increased at the end of the 19th century, with the opening of the Manchester Canal, which turned the city into a major seaport. Industrial areas are located near the port berths and along the railways.
